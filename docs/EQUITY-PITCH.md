From our conversation, the skills matrix discussion is coming. When you sit down pre-revenue and ask "what do we need to trade a finished product?", the answers will include:

- Website and digital presence (already underway)
- E-commerce platform
- CRM and lead management automation
- Patient/practitioner portals
- Marketing automation
- Data and analytics infrastructure
- AI-powered automation and workflows

Neither of you are technical builders. Hiring a senior full-stack developer or fractional CTO in this market costs $180–250/hr, or $150–200k/year full-time. You're getting that capability at a fraction of the cost.

### 2. I'm Not Just a Builder — I'm a Product Person

My background isn't just writing code. I've spent years as an **Engineering Manager and Technical Lead** across companies like SEEK, Belong, and Catch — leading product delivery, shaping roadmaps, and building the systems that scale businesses.

What I bring beyond code:

| Capability | Value to Revivum |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Product management** | Shape what you build and in what order — prioritise by impact, not gut feel |
| **Technical architecture** | Design systems that scale from 10 users to 10,000 without rewriting everything |
| **Automation & AI** | Automate manual processes (lead triage, patient comms, practitioner onboarding) as the business grows |

### 3. Aligned Incentives

Cash-only arrangements mean I'm a vendor. Equity means I'm invested in the outcome.

With equity:
- I'll proactively suggest improvements I see, not just build what's asked
- I'll think about long-term architecture, not just quick wins
- I'll be available for quick calls and advice beyond scoped work
- I'll care about your CAC, conversion rates, and retention — not just whether the code works
- I have skin in the game when things get hard

### 4. The Industry Timing Is Right

You said it yourself — if cannabis is a billion-dollar industry in Australia, peptides will be 2-3x that. First movers who get the digital infrastructure right will compound that advantage.

I want to be part of that because:

## Proposed Structure

### Option A — Simple Sweat Equity (Preferred)

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equity** | 5% of Revivum Pty Ltd |
| **Vesting** | 2-year vesting, 6-month cliff |
| **Cash compensation** | Standard consulting rate for scoped work (per services agreement) |
| **Role** | Fractional Head of Product & Technology |
| **Time commitment** | ~5-10 hrs/week beyond scoped project work |

Vesting protects you — if I disappear after 3 months, I don't keep the full 5%. It also protects me — it formalises the arrangement so there's no ambiguity later.

### Option B — Reduced Rate + Equity

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equity** | 5% of Revivum Pty Ltd |
| **Vesting** | 2-year vesting, 6-month cliff |
| **Cash compensation** | Reduced rate ($100/hr vs $150/hr) for scoped work |
| **Role** | Fractional Head of Product & Technology |
| **Time commitment** | ~5-10 hrs/week beyond scoped project work |

This option reduces your cash outlay by ~33% on project work while I take more risk alongside you.
